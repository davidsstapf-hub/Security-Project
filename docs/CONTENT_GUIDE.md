# Content Authoring Guide

## Principles

- Teach the concept; do not merely optimize for memorizing an answer.
- Use original wording and examples.
- Distinguish official objective coverage from our interpretation or teaching advice.
- Prefer current primary sources such as standards bodies, government guidance, and vendor documentation.
- Label difficulty and the objective covered by every question.
- Explain why each option is correct or incorrect.

## Proposed identifiers

Use stable, readable IDs:

```text
domain-1
domain-1-controls
domain-1-controls-lesson-01
domain-1-controls-question-001
```

## Review checklist

- Technically accurate and current
- Mapped to an official objective
- Original and free of exam-dump material
- Accessible language with acronyms expanded on first use
- Includes a useful explanation and realistic context
- Reviewed whenever the exam version changes
