# SY0-701 Curriculum Map

This is the app's high-level curriculum map. Each topic will eventually link to lessons, flashcards, practice questions, and scenario exercises. The official CompTIA exam objectives remain the authoritative source.

## 1. General Security Concepts — 12%

- Categories and functions of security controls
- Confidentiality, integrity, and availability
- Authentication, authorization, and accounting
- Zero trust, least privilege, and separation of duties
- Change management and its security impact
- Public key infrastructure, encryption, hashing, signing, and obfuscation

## 2. Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Mitigations — 22%

- Threat actors, motivations, and attributes
- Common threat vectors and attack surfaces
- Application, operating-system, cloud, mobile, network, and supply-chain vulnerabilities
- Social engineering and physical attacks
- Malware, password attacks, network attacks, and application attacks
- Segmentation, access controls, hardening, isolation, and other mitigations

## 3. Security Architecture — 18%

- On-premises, cloud, hybrid, virtualization, IoT, ICS, and embedded architectures
- Secure enterprise infrastructure and network design
- Data classification, sovereignty, encryption, tokenization, and lifecycle protection
- High availability, redundancy, backups, continuity, and disaster recovery
- Architecture tradeoffs and shared-responsibility considerations

## 4. Security Operations — 28%

- Secure baselines and hardening for enterprise assets
- Asset inventory and lifecycle management
- Vulnerability scanning, analysis, prioritization, and remediation
- Logging, alerting, monitoring, and security tooling
- Identity and access management operations
- Automation and orchestration
- Incident response, digital forensics, evidence handling, and root-cause analysis
- Data sources used in investigations

## 5. Security Program Management and Oversight — 20%

- Policies, standards, procedures, governance, and roles
- Risk identification, analysis, treatment, reporting, and registers
- Third-party and supply-chain risk management
- Compliance, privacy, audits, assessments, and penetration testing
- Security awareness, phishing simulations, and behavior change
- Business impact analysis and program metrics

## Suggested content unit

Every lesson should include:

1. objective and prerequisites;
2. plain-language explanation;
3. concrete technical example;
4. common misconception or exam trap;
5. key terms and flashcards;
6. knowledge-check questions with answer explanations;
7. one scenario or decision exercise;
8. links to reputable primary references.
